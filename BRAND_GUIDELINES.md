## 📖 ARTICLE FORMULA (EVERY ARTICLE)

### STRUCTURE (REQUIRED):

**1. Hook (first 100 words)**
- Start with a problem overthinkers face
- Make it personal ("You've probably felt this...")
- Ask a question that makes them lean in

Example:
> "You're lying in bed at 2am, replaying a conversation from 6 hours ago. You said something awkward. They definitely noticed. Your whole relationship is probably ruined now. Welcome to the overthinking spiral."

**2. The Concept (200-300 words)**
- What is this idea?
- Why does it matter?
- How does it apply to overthinkers specifically?
- Make it concrete, not abstract

**3. Why It Works (300 words)**
- 2-3 specific reasons
- Real examples (not hypothetical)
- Show the cause-and-effect

**4. The Action (EXACTLY 4 SENTENCES, <10 MINUTES)**
- Step 1 (specific, not vague)
- Step 2 (build on step 1)
- Step 3 (final piece)
- Closing (how to know it worked)

Example:
> "Notice one thing you're overthinking right now. Write it down in one sentence. Ask yourself: 'Can I control this?' If no, stop thinking about it—literally say 'stop' out loud. If yes, write down ONE action you can take today and do it within 1 hour."

**5. Closing Line (REQUIRED - ALWAYS)**
> "That's enough for today."

## ✍️ WRITING RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

### Length
- Minimum: 1,200 words
- Maximum: 2,000 words
- Target: 1,400 words
- Why: Long enough to be credible, short enough to finish in one sitting

### Paragraphs
- Max 3 sentences per paragraph
- Short sentences (avg 10-12 words)
- Use line breaks liberally (white space = readable)

### Examples
- Every concept needs 1-2 real examples
- Examples should be relatable (not famous people)
- "Like when you..." (personal, specific)

### No Fluff
- Every sentence adds value
- No padding, no filler
- If you delete it and nothing breaks, delete it

### Voice
- First person okay ("I used to...")
- Second person great ("You know that feeling...")
- But NEVER condescending ("Let me explain...")

## 🔍 SEO REQUIREMENTS (PER ARTICLE)

### Meta Title (55-60 characters)
- Include primary keyword
- Benefit-driven
- No clickbait

Example: "Stop Wasting Energy on What You Can't Control"

### Meta Description (150-160 characters)
- Summary of article
- Include CTA
- Natural language

Example: "Learn what you can and can't control. One Stoic idea that stops overthinking. Read: 5 min. Action: 10 min."

### H-Structure
- H1: Article title (once only)
- H2: Section headers (2-4 per article)
- H3: Sub-points (as needed)
- No skipping levels (H1 → H2, not H1 → H3)

### Alt Text (Images)
- Descriptive, not generic
- Include keyword naturally
- Example: "Concept visualization: attention is a choice"

### Schema Markup
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Article",
  "headline": "Article Title",
  "description": "Meta description",
  "author": {
    "@type": "Person",
    "name": "Claude"
  },
  "datePublished": "2026-06-03",
  "image": "header-image-url",
  "articleBody": "..."
}
```

### Keywords
- Primary keyword: In title, first 100 words, one H2
- Secondary keywords: Naturally in body (2-3 mentions)
- Long-tail keywords: In headings and examples
- NO keyword stuffing
