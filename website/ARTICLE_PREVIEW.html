Stop Waiting For Permission

7 min read
April 10, 2026
1,847 words

You're waiting for someone to tell you it's okay. That you're good enough. That you have permission to begin.

That person isn't coming.

Permission isn't something someone gives you. It's something you give yourself. And most of us have been trained to believe we need external validation before we can act. A degree. A job title. A mentor's approval. A lucky break.

The Stoics had a different view. Marcus Aurelius didn't wait for the Senate to tell him he was ready to lead. Epictetus didn't wait for freedom before claiming his own mind. They understood something we've forgotten:

You have more permission than you think. You're just afraid to use it.

The Permission Trap

We ask for permission because it feels safer. If you fail, it's not your fault—you were just following instructions. If you succeed, someone else approved, so you deserved it.

But this is a trade. You trade autonomy for safety. And the safety is an illusion.

The person you're waiting for isn't going to validate all your choices. They can't. They don't know what you need to become.

What Permission Actually Means

Real permission is internal. It's the decision to stop waiting and start acting with what you have.

You don't need permission to:

  • Start learning something new
  • Have an opinion that's different
  • Make a decision about your own time
  • Change your mind when evidence changes
  • Say no to things that don't align with your values

These are already yours. The only person who needs to give you permission is you.

Today's action

Identify one decision you're sitting on because you're waiting for permission. Make it today. Write down what you decided and why. (4 minutes.)

That's enough for today.
